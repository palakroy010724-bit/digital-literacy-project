project **OVERVIEW**:
<br>
<br>
This has been divided into five practical benchmarks:
<br>
<br>
• **Design**: We begin by producing a powerful infographic that transforms difficult digital ideas into understandable, graphic narratives. 
<br>
• **Coding**: By delving into the Foundations of Coding, we'll reveal the inner workings of the websites and applications we use on a daily basis.
<br>
 • **Communication**: We'll become experts in email etiquette, making sure that every communication you write to a professor or potential employer is respected. 
 <br>
• **Professionalism**: You'll create a digital portfolio, a professional home base that presents the world with your greatest work and abilities. 
<br>
• **Protection:** Lastly, we address the internet's wild west by learning how to recognise and prevent cybercrime, which includes identity theft and phishing.
<br>
You will be using the internet with a sense of purpose, security, and professional advantage at the end of this project.
<br>
<br>
<br>
**MODULE-1:**
This module will look at visual story-telling, using widely used, industry standard, and industry accepted resources. Through this, you will learn how to use both Canva to design professional, graphically enhanced presentations and documents, Prezi for highly effective presentations and Cooltext to provide professional, stylised text effects. Using all of these, learners can ensure their information has a maximum impact when presented in a visual format.
<br><br>
**MODULE-2:**
This section addresses professional branding by helping students develop a unified online portfolio. Students will learn what kind of projects to include, how to showcase their accomplishments, and which platforms to use to host their portfolios. This final document is designed to serve as a live, evolving work, that will attract and engage employers.
<br><br>
**MODULE-3:**
In this module we discuss the core ecosystems used in modern development. We look at coding platforms and communities for version control and knowledge sharing such as Gitub and Kaggle, the benefits of contributing to open source projects, and team working as part of a dispersed tech team.
<br><br>
**MODULE-4:**
With clear and unambiguous communication being paramount in the digital age, the section on composing professional emails explains what is appropriate in terms of tone, layout and clarity. It also teaches responsible online networking, particularly the understanding of the etiquette involved in social networking sites and how to do it both professionally and culturally appropriately.
<br><br>
**MODULE-5:**
In this essential safety module, the students will explore the types of new, digital dangers they face, such as phishing, identification theft and financial fraud. Using practical case studies the students will identify how to prevent it by recognizing warning signs, setting up preventive strategies and adopting safe computing behaviors to keep their and their organizations data secure in today's connected world.
