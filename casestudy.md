

**Case Study:** *The Corporate Credentials Harvest* 

**Phishing** is a social engineering attack whereby attackers impersonate a trustworthy entity, such as a bank, common service, or a company's CEO to steal credentials, credit card numbers, or proprietary corporate data. 

Phishing attack stages:

&#x20;The phishing attack usually follows a four-step process: 

1. The **Bait**: The attacker sends out a spoofed communication (usually an e-mail) which will aim to put pressure on the victim and convince the victim into believing their account is at risk, "Your account will be deactivated in 24 hours due to a security breach." 
2. The **Hook**: This e-mail will contain a link that takes the victim to a 'secure portal.' The 'secure portal' is a visually exact replica of a website such as the Microsoft 365 login page.
3. The **Catch:** The victim feels pressured into keying in their login details into the fake portal. 
4. The **Exploit:** The credentials are captured in real-time and used either to get through corporate firewalls and gain access or to sell access on the black market. 

Target Audience \& Resulting Action:

Phishing attacks will target everyone, however some attacks will be more effective if targeting University students or employees of a corporation as this gives the attackers a way to get at a more valuable network of accounts. 

In a real scenario a student receives an email from a spoofed 'University IT support.' The consequence is severe as it leads to identity theft, compromise of personal academic accounts and gives the attacker an easy way to spam the university with spam and malicious emails.

&#x20;The FACC scam of 2016 shows this with $47m lost to a 'CEO fraud' phishing email in the aerospace company (Source: Reuters).
