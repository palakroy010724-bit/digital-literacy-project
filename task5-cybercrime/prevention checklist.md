When the academic world is entirely online, merely using a good password isn't sufficient protection. Since in India the world of UPI and digital transactions are the norm for students-from canteen payments to receiving semester fee, one must exercise certain unique precautions.



**Student's 'Stay Safe Online' Checklist:**

* **Make Accounts secure with MFA:** Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on all your university emails, GitHub, social media etc. An individual, even after stealing your password, won't be able to access the account without the second verification.
* **Verify UPI 'Request Money' scams:** Remember, one only has to enter the UPI PIN when sending money and never when receiving it. If one is requested a certain sum of money saying it is for a refund or as a prize money, decline the request.
* **Check app permissions:** Frequently examine which apps are gaining access to your microphone, contacts, and location, and remove any "cleaner" or "battery saver" type of apps, that can be a gateway to malware.
* **Download APK from original source:** Don't download "modded" or pirated apps for your Btech projects. Stay away from such versions, as they can be a means of data theft through trojans. The right source is the Google Play Store or any developer site.
* G**uard your QR code information:** Be careful when scanning QR codes at any public place. They could be replaced by those placed by fraudsters, that could lead you to losing your hard earned money. Verify the name on the screen of your transaction page before you initiate the payment.
* **Secure your digital profile:** When uploading your projects on various platforms such as GitHub or Kaggle, you need to ensure that you aren't accidentally uploading any of your "secrets" (your API key or contact info) in your code comments.
* **Fake 'job/internship' offers:** Any kind of unsolicited WA or Telegram message offering a hefty amount of pay, stating that you will get paid after furnishing a "security deposit", is a fake offer. No professional entity would ever charge money for employing a candidate.
* **Public Wi-Fi caution:** If you are in a cafe or a railway station and using a free public Wi-Fi, do not access your banking or student portal login details. Instead, use a reliable VPN to encrypt your connection.



**How to report Cyber Crime in India:**

If you or someone you know has been a victim of any kind of cyber/financial crime, reporting it immediately is of paramount importance, as it can lead to blocking of illegal transactions, freezing and recovering the fraudulent money.



National Cyber Crime Helpline: **1930**

National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: Visit **cybercrime.gov.in**
